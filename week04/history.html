History of Korean Dog Breeds

Korean dogs have been companions to people for centuries, bred for hunting, guarding, and even spiritual protection. These breeds are known for their intelligence, loyalty, and strong survival instincts. While some, like the Jindo, have gained international fame, others remain rare and deeply tied to Korean heritage.

Jindo (진돗개)

The Jindo dog comes from Jindo Island and is known for its incredible loyalty and intelligence. Originally bred for hunting, these dogs are fearless and quick learners. Today, Jindos are a national treasure in Korea and are often used for search and rescue missions.

Pungsan (풍산개)

Originally from North Korea, the Pungsan dog was bred to hunt large game in snowy mountains. These dogs are known for their strength, endurance, and independent nature. Despite their fierce hunting skills, they form deep bonds with their owners and are highly protective.

Donggyeongi (동경이)

The Donggyeongi is a rare breed from Gyeongju, easily recognized by its naturally short or missing tail. These dogs were once favored by Korean royalty and were used for hunting and guarding. Today, efforts are being made to preserve this ancient breed.

Sapsali (삽살개)

The Sapsali, often called the "ghost-chasing dog," was believed to have spiritual powers that could drive away evil spirits. With their long, shaggy fur and friendly nature, they were historically owned by Korean nobility. After near extinction during wars, dedicated efforts have helped revive this breed.

Jeju Dog (제주개)

Native to Jeju Island, the Jeju Dog is a strong and independent breed, originally used for guarding homes and hunting. These dogs are highly intelligent and were bred to survive the island’s harsh terrain. Though rare today, preservation programs are working to protect this historic breed.
