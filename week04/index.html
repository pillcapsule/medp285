History of Breeds

Korean dog breeds have been around for centuries, helping people hunt, guard homes, and even protect against spirits. The Jindo is the most famous, known for its loyalty, while the Pungsan from North Korea was bred for hunting in cold mountains. Other rare breeds like the Sapsali, Jeju Dog, and Donggyeongi have deep roots in Korean history but are less known outside the country.

Skills

Korean dogs were bred for specific skills that helped people survive. Jindos became expert hunters and loyal guardians, while Pungsans learned to track prey in snowy mountains. The shaggy Sapsali was believed to chase away evil spirits, and the Jeju Dog protected homes from wild animals. Even the rare Donggyeongi, with its bobtail, was valued for hunting and guarding. Over time, these dogs kept their strong instincts, loyalty, and intelligence, making them special even today.

Attitude

Korean dogs are known for their loyalty, intelligence, and bravery. Jindos are especially devoted to their owners and can be wary of strangers. Pungsans are strong and independent but form deep bonds with their families. Sapsalis are friendly and gentle, while Jeju Dogs and Donggyeongis are alert and protective.
